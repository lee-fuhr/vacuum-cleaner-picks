How we choose our picks

We don’t do in-house vacuum torture tests. Instead, we comb through thousands of verified owner reviews (especially the ones written six months or a year after purchase), cross-reference published tests from sources that run standardized carpet and hard-floor trials, and track repair forums to see which parts fail most often. A vacuum that scores high in a lab but gets buried in complaints about a snapped belt or a lost handle latch doesn’t make our list. We also pay attention to what real people say about ease of use: how often do you have to unclog the hose, how simple is it to change the bag or empty the bin, does the brush roll stop working after a few months. Our picks have stood up to those long-term signals and have a strong track record for a reason.
